## Rationale

### Why Class 1

The STRATA Protocol methodology has been developed through direct engineering practice. However, the *platform* — the website, the membership system, the templates, the prompts, and the documentation — has never been built, published, or offered to any customer. There are no paying users. There are no contractual obligations. There is no existing system to preserve.

Class 1 is correct because the business is still discovering and validating what form the product takes in the market: Who pays? What do they pay for? What level of template depth produces adoption?

### Why Platformization (1.3), not Greenfield MVP (1.1)

A Greenfield MVP is the right category when the *business hypothesis itself* is unvalidated. Here, the STRATA methodology is the validated asset — internally proven through engineering practice. What this initiative does is *convert* that validated internal capability into an accessible, scalable, externally-facing platform.

The Platformization category applies when: "APIs become products, internal tooling becomes infrastructure, systems become multi-tenant." In this case: a methodology becomes a documentation platform, internal templates become a member toolkit, and a personal engineering practice becomes a framework accessible to thousands of practitioners.

Platformization also dictates the correct engineering priorities for this initiative:
- **Abstraction**: The framework must generalize across project types
- **Developer experience**: Members and visitors must find it fast, clear, and actionable
- **Interoperability**: Templates must work with GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and Claude equally
- **Reusability**: The content structure must allow expansion without architectural rewrites

### What this classification prohibits

A Greenfield MVP classification would have produced: a thinner MVP, less structured content architecture, and a template system too shallow to be useful. That would be wrong.

A Greenfield MVP classification would permit: significant technical debt in the content system, rough navigation, and placeholder-heavy documentation. For a methodology platform that demonstrates best-in-class documentation, those are not acceptable trade-offs.

## Continuity Constraints

None at launch.

**Reclassification note:** When the platform acquires its first paying members, a reclassification assessment is required. At that point, template deprecations, URL changes, and authentication changes become continuity risks. The platform transitions toward Class 2 territory and the engineering strategy must reflect that.

---

## Risk of Misclassification

**Primary risk:** Treating this as a Greenfield MVP and underinvesting in content architecture, template depth, and documentation quality. The result: a platform that cannot serve practitioners, eroding the credibility signal of STRATA's self-referential proof.

**Secondary risk:** Over-engineering the platform before market validation. STRATA Platform-First principles apply: build the minimum platform that serves practitioners now; extend when demand proves it.
